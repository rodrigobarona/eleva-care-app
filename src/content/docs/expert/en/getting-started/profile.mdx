# Setting Up Your Profile

Your profile is your digital presence on Eleva Care. A complete, professional profile helps patients find you and builds trust before they book.

## Profile Elements

### Required Information

These fields must be completed before you can publish:

| Field | Description | Tips |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Display Name** | Your professional name | Use your name with credentials (e.g., "Dr. Maria Santos, PT, PhD") |
| **Profile Photo** | Professional headshot | High-quality, friendly, professional attire |
| **Bio** | 100-1000 characters | Highlight your expertise and approach |
| **Specializations** | Areas of expertise | Select all that apply |
| **Languages** | Languages you speak | Important for international patients |

### Optional Information

Enhance your profile with:

- Years of experience
- Education and certifications
- Professional associations
- Location (city/country)
- Social media links (LinkedIn, website)

## Writing Your Bio

Your bio is often the first thing patients read. Make it count.

### Structure

1. **Opening** - Your specialty and experience
2. **Approach** - How you work with patients
3. **Background** - Key credentials and training
4. **Personal Touch** - Why you're passionate about this work

### Example Bio

> "I'm a pelvic floor physiotherapist with 12 years of experience helping women regain confidence and quality of life. I specialize in pregnancy preparation, postpartum recovery, and treating pelvic organ prolapse.
>
> My approach combines evidence-based techniques with compassionate care, ensuring each patient feels heard and supported throughout their journey.
>
> I hold a PhD in Women's Health Physiotherapy from the University of Lisbon and am certified in biofeedback and manual therapy techniques."

<Callout type="tip">
  **Pro Tip**: Write in first person ("I specialize in...") to create a personal connection with potential patients.
</Callout>

## Profile Photo Guidelines

Your photo significantly impacts patient trust. Follow these guidelines:

### Do's

- ✅ Use a recent, high-resolution photo
- ✅ Dress professionally (as you would for a consultation)
- ✅ Choose a neutral or professional background
- ✅ Smile naturally - appear approachable
- ✅ Ensure good lighting (natural light works best)

### Don'ts

- ❌ Use group photos or photos with others
- ❌ Use heavily filtered or edited photos
- ❌ Use casual vacation or social photos
- ❌ Use logos or graphics instead of your photo

## Selecting Specializations

Choose specializations that accurately reflect your expertise:

### Primary Categories

- **Pregnancy & Birth** - Prenatal care, birth preparation
- **Postpartum** - Recovery, breastfeeding support
- **Pelvic Health** - Incontinence, prolapse, pelvic pain
- **Menopause** - Hormonal health, symptom management
- **Sexual Health** - Dysfunction, counseling
- **Mental Health** - Perinatal mental health, anxiety, depression

### Sub-specializations

Each category has specific sub-specializations. Select only those where you have training and experience.

<Callout type="warning">
  Only select specializations where you're qualified to provide care. Patients trust that listed specializations represent your actual expertise.
</Callout>

## Credentials & Verification

### Required Documents

To publish your profile, you must verify:

1. **Identity** - Government-issued ID
2. **Professional License** - Current, valid license in your field
3. **Insurance** - Proof of professional liability coverage

### Verification Process

1. Upload documents in your dashboard
2. Our team reviews within 2-3 business days
3. Receive confirmation email when approved
4. Profile eligible for publication

## Publishing Your Profile

Once all required elements are complete:

1. Go to **Expert Dashboard** > **Profile**
2. Review all information
3. Click **Publish Profile**
4. Your profile becomes visible to patients

### Unpublishing

You can unpublish your profile at any time:

1. Go to **Profile Settings**
2. Toggle **Profile Visibility** off
3. Existing appointments are not affected

## Editing Your Profile

You can update your profile anytime:

1. Go to **Expert Dashboard** > **Profile**
2. Click **Edit** on any section
3. Make your changes
4. Click **Save**

Changes are reflected immediately on your public profile.

## Best Practices

### Stand Out From Others

- Use a unique, memorable bio
- Highlight specific training or certifications
- Mention languages spoken
- Include your approach to patient care

### Keep It Updated

- Review your profile quarterly
- Update credentials when renewed
- Add new specializations as you gain expertise
- Refresh your photo every 2-3 years
